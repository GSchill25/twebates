Peer to Peer

Bitcoin is a truly independent global payment network. It's protocol allows users to send payments from one account to another anywhere in the world almost instantaneously. While major US banking transactions usually take 1-3 business days, Bitcoin's peer to peer protocol offers a fast alternative.

Decentralized

Bitcoin's network is made up of all the individual user's computers who are making transactions. It is not controlled by a central bank, single government, or any government for that matter. This allows transactions fees to be minimal compared to those issued by creditors with traditonal dollars and other currencies.

Wallets

Bitcoin wallets are digital storage places for your bitcoin. They are addressed by a long unique hash like "aj234lnlk5609sn23kfg023". This hash is safe to give out and you will need to for others to send bitcoin to your wallet. Bitcoin wallet software can be downloaded to your local device and this is a very secure way to store your coin. Wallets exist on websites also, but they are at the liberty of the security of that individual website. "Private" or "Local" wallets are much more secure.

BlockChain

The blockchain is a history of every bitcoin transaction ever made. It can be thought of as a public ledger. The blockchain keeps track of what account a payment comes from, the account it is going to, the amount sent, and a number of other details.
